# Early Warning System for European Banking Vulnerabilities
 
A data-driven Early Warning System (EWS) for detecting capital adequacy vulnerabilities across European banks, built on publicly available EBA supervisory data.
 
---
 
## Overview
 
This project develops an end-to-end analytical pipeline to assess the resilience of the European banking sector and identify institutions at risk of capital deterioration. Using the **EBA EU-wide Transparency Exercise 2025** dataset, covering **119 banks across 25 countries** over four quarterly periods (September 2024 – June 2025),the analysis combines exploratory data analysis, unsupervised clustering, a supervised early warning model, and geographical visualisation.
 
The project is structured as a series of six notebooks, each corresponding to a distinct analytical step.

## Data Source
 
**EBA EU-wide Transparency Exercise 2025**, publicly available at [eba.europa.eu](https://www.eba.europa.eu)
 
- **Coverage:** 119 European banks, 25 countries
- **Periods:** September 2024, December 2024, March 2025, June 2025
- **Key indicators extracted:** CET1 Capital, Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA), CET1 Ratio, Total Assets
---
 
## Methodology
 
| Step | Notebook | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Data Loading & Cleaning | Extract key indicators from raw EBA data, handle missing values, compute CET1 ratio |
| 2 | Exploratory Analysis | Distributional analysis, outlier treatment, time-series trends by country, bank-level vulnerability ranking |
| 3 | Clustering | PCA (2 components) + K-Means (k=3) to group banks by financial profile |
| 4 | Early Warning Model | Logistic Regression and Random Forest to predict which banks fall below the 15% CET1 caution threshold in the following quarter |
| 5 | Results & Policy Implications | Synthesis of findings, macroprudential discussion, country-level vulnerability overview |
| 6 | Geographical Analysis | Choropleth maps of average CET1 ratios and vulnerability shares by country |
 
---
 
## Key Findings
 
- **The European banking sector is broadly resilient.** No bank in the sample falls below the 8% regulatory minimum. The average CET1 ratio is 18.6%.
- **Spain is the most capital-constrained country**, with 8 out of 10 banks below the 15% caution threshold and an average CET1 of 14.1%.
- **Three distinct bank clusters** emerge: standard small/medium institutions (n=92), large systemic banks (n=8), and high-capital specialised institutions (n=5).
- **The early warning model achieves 95% accuracy and 80% recall** for at-risk banks using Logistic Regression, selected for its higher AUC and interpretability in a supervisory context.
- **A geographical east–west gradient** is visible, with Eastern European banking systems generally showing higher capital ratios than western peripheral economies.

## Usage
 
1. Download the EBA Transparency Exercise 2025 dataset from [eba.europa.eu](https://www.eba.europa.eu) and place `tr_oth.csv` and `TR_Metadata.xlsx` in the project root.
2. Run the notebooks in order, starting from `01_data_loading_cleaning.ipynb`.
3. The cleaned dataset (`banking_indicators_clean.csv`) is generated by notebook 01 and used by all subsequent notebooks.
---
 
## Limitations
 
- The model's high AUC is partly driven by CET1 autocorrelation: institutions with low capital ratios today tend to remain low in the short term
- Only four quarterly observations are available, limiting the scope for dynamic modelling
- The leverage ratio was excluded due to excessive missing values (>50%)
